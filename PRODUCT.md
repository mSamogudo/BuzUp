# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Public-transport passengers in Mozambique — daily commuters (workers, students), families, and tourists — plus fleet operators and municipalities as secondary audiences. Primary context: mobile-first, on the go, often one-handed, sometimes on slow connections. Primary language Portuguese (pt), secondary English (en). Most arrive on the landing page to understand what BusUp is and download the app or get a card; a smaller set lands on pricing (bilhetes/tarifas) or contact.

## Product Purpose

BusUp is a contactless payment platform for public transport in Mozambique (a UpDigital product). Tap a card or phone at the on-board validator to pay in under a second; recharge via M-Pesa, e-Mola, or bank card; buy daily/weekly/monthly digital tickets and track balance, trips, and history in real time. The public marketing surface exists to convert visitors into app downloads / card sign-ups and to establish trust that a tap-to-pay transit product is fast, safe, and legitimate. Success = downloads, card requests, operator/municipality leads.

## Brand Personality

Fast, secure, modern. Fintech-confident and trustworthy without being cold; efficient and reassuring at the payment/money moments. Blue-and-white identity (brand blue #0069E9, deep navy #06122F). Voice: clear, direct, benefit-led, bilingual PT/EN. Should feel like a product you'd trust with your money and your daily commute.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS template** — no cookie-cutter startup landing: avoid identical icon+heading+text card grids repeated endlessly, an uppercase tracked eyebrow above every section, the hero-metric cliché, purple-gradient-on-white.
- **Crypto / neon fintech** — no dark-neon hype aesthetic, no glow-maximalism, no "to the moon" energy. Trust, not speculation.
- **Government / bureaucratic** — no stiff institutional public-sector transit look; modern consumer product, not a municipal portal.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust is the product.** Every money/payment moment (recharge, tap-to-pay, ticket purchase) must feel safe and certain. Reassurance beats cleverness.
2. **Mobile-first, thumb-first.** The real user is one-handed on a phone, possibly on 3G. Primary actions reachable, assets light, state preserved.
3. **Speak plainly, bilingually.** PT primary, EN secondary; benefit-led copy over jargon. A first-time transit rider must understand the first action within seconds.
4. **Accessible to the whole city.** Students, families, tourists, older riders — the design serves people who have never used tap-to-pay, not just the tech-savvy.
5. **Confident, not loud.** Distinctive and committed to the blue identity, but calm where money is involved. Bold in hero and imagery, quiet at the transaction.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast (≥3:1 large), full keyboard navigation with visible focus, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all reveal/scroll motion, meaningful alt text (bilingual voice), 44×44px minimum touch targets, no meaning by color alone. Bilingual PT/EN with a language toggle. Must work on low-end Android and slow connections.
